- **Cannot perceive:** Width, height, depth.

### **2D (Second Dimension)**
- **Definition:** A plane. Points require 2 coordinates (x, y) to locate.
- **Visual:** A flat surface, like a piece of paper.

## 🎲 GEOMETRIC OBJECTS

### **Tesseract (Hypercube)**
- **Definition:** A 4D analog of a cube. A cube extended along the 4th axis (w).
- **Visual in 3D:** Appears as a small cube inside a larger cube with connecting lines.
- **Properties:**
  - 16 vertices (corners)
  - 32 edges
  - 24 square faces
  - 8 cubic cells
- **Citation:** "A 3D cube extended along the 4th axis becomes a tesseract."

---

### **Hypersphere**
- **Definition:** A 4D analog of a sphere.
- **Cross-section in 3D:** A sphere that appears from nowhere, grows, shrinks, vanishes.
- **Analogy:** Like a 3D sphere passing through 2D appears as a circle that grows and shrinks.

---

### **Cross-Section**
- **Definition:** The shape created when a higher-dimensional object passes through a lower-dimensional space.
- **Example:** A 3D sphere passing through 2D creates a circular cross-section.
- **Example:** A 4D hypersphere passing through 3D creates a spherical cross-section.
- **Quote:** "If you 'slide' a cube through the 4th dimension, its 3D cross-sections would look like changing shapes."
